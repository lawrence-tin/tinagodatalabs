Part 3: Standard Report Sections (The Shared Framework)
Regardless of report type, all social media reports should include these core sections :

1. Executive Summary (2-3 sentences)
A snapshot of top findings and highlights. Connect social activities to business goals immediately.

2. Metrics and KPIs
Track quantifiable performance against goals. Include both engagement metrics (rate, comments, shares) and business metrics (conversions, ROAS).

3. Performance Analysis
Identify trends, wins, and areas to improve. Explain WHY results happened — not just what happened.

4. Audience Insights
Understand who's engaging and why. Include demographics, active times, interests, and audience quality indicators.

5. Content Performance Analysis
Show which content types, formats, and topics generate the most reach, engagement, and conversions.

6. Platform Performance
Compare results across social networks. Each platform plays a distinct role — help teams allocate resources effectively.

7. Recommendations
Outline clear next steps based on findings. Management cares about "what should we DO about this?"
